The Myriad Threats

Hackers lurk. Scammers pounce. Corporations profile. Ex-friends and ex-lovers hold grudges. Governments surveil. The digital stage is busy, and the audience isn't always friendly.

Most people navigate this carnival of risks as Heroes—hearts full, believing their sincerity protects them. But sincerity alone doesn't stop data brokers, phishing kits, or exploitative legal processes that systematically prey on the trusting, the earnest, and the poor.

Enter the Zerophile: a person who leaks zero data except when they want to. They aren't always cynical, and they aren't always paranoid, but their pride in their SSecPPrideRR is fundamentally unwavering.

What is SSecPPrideRR?

SSecPPrideRR refers to the eight pillars of digital sovereignty:

  • Searchability: it's easy to find your own data.
  • Security: intrusion is difficult, damage-controlled, and detectable.
  • Provability: if you last edited a specific file on a specific day, you can prove that.
  • Privacy: you only leak data and metadata that you consent to leaking.
  • Deletability: data that seems gone, really is.
  • Deniability: you can plausibly deny storing a file that you're storing.
  • Reliability: your systems keep working day-after-day, without endless repairs or technical 'fiddling'.
  • Recoverability: your data is backed up remotely, and thus recoverable when things go wrong.

Together, the eight pillars support a level of digital sovereignty that most professionals consider out of reach for regular folk.
