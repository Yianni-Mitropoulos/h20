The Myriad Threats

Hackers lurk. Scammers pounce. Corporations profile. Ex-friends and ex-lovers hold grudges. Governments surveil. The digital stage is busy, and the audience isn’t always friendly.

Most people walk through this carnival of risks as heroes: hearts full, convinced their sincerity protects them. But sincerity alone doesn’t stop data brokers, phishing kits, or legal processes that systematically prey on the trusting, the earnest, and the poor.

Enter the “zerophile”: a person who leaks zero data except when they want to. They aren't always cynical, and they aren't always paranoid, but their pride in their SecPride is fundamentally unwavering.

What is SecPride?

SecPride refers to the four pillars of digital sovereignty: Security, Privacy, Deletability, and Deniability. Together, they build a sovereignty posture that most professionals consider out of reach for regular folk. We say otherwise.

For some, it's a hobby. For others, it's a way of life. SecPride isn’t about hiding in bunkers. It’s about dignity, autonomy, and having fun while you keep your digital skin intact.

The Four Pillars of Digital Sovereignty

The four pillars can be illustrated by reference to homeowner sovereignty.

  1. Security

    Prevent unauthorized access to your home.

    Ensure individuals in your home can only access the appropriate rooms.

  2. Privacy

    Prevent people peeking in through your windows.

    Prevent individuals in one room from hearing conversations in another.

  3. Deletability

    When you throw an item in the bin, you know it's gone for good.

  4. Deniability

    Whether or not any given item is actually in your home remains unclear to anyone but you — even if there's a breach.
