Key Management

Our GPG private key is the beating heart of Hero-to-Zero's trust model. Here's how it's handled:

  • Air-gapped computer: permanently stripped of all network hardware.
  • Encrypted key: the private key is always encrypted, even on the air-gapped system.
  • Vault storage: the system lives in a tamper-detectable, RF-shielded secure vault.

Physical Safeguards

  • Backups are separated from active signing media.
  • Access to the vault requires dual presence; media are serialized.
  • No radios, NICs, or wireless modules — zero connectivity by design.

Operational Controls

  • The passphrase is known only to one person — Yianni, our founder.
  • The key is only unlocked for short, bounded signing sessions.
  • Transfers in and out are done with single-purpose, wiped media.
  • Policy changes require out-of-band approval and full documentation.

Residual Risk

The one big gap we can't solve yet: compelled backdoors by court order. A legal mandate could force the introduction of tampered software or processes.

For now, our small size makes this an unlikely threat in the medium term. But in the long run, it's a problem that demands a stronger answer.

Roadmap

We're exploring distributed trust models that reduce single points of failure and resist compelled subversion:

  • Threshold signing (keys split across independent custodians).
  • Reproducible builds (verifiable artifacts).
  • Transparency logs (auditable signing sessions).

We haven't yet found a design that's both practical and resilient. Please give us time — progress is underway.

FAQ

Q: Why not use an HSM?
A: HSMs reduce some risks, but they don't eliminate compelled backdoors and introduce new supply-chain trust issues.

Q: What if Yianni is unavailable?
A: We have continuity procedures, but will only detail them alongside the distributed-trust rollout to avoid opening new attack surfaces prematurely.
